My front 4 consists of Ronaldo, Bale, Di Maria, and Ibrahimovic. I'll breakdown why each player earned a spot in my best XI.

Cristiano Ronaldo's performance as a left winger during the 2013/2014 UEFA Champions League season was absolutely devastating. With 17 goals and 5 assists, totaling 22 combined goals and assists, a UCL record, he showcased his versatility and impact on the team. His remarkable goals per 90 minutes ratio of 1.54 underscored his goal-scoring prowess. He's seen at the top of many of these graphs, which just typifies how he's been at the top of this sport for so so long. Ronaldo's presence on the left wing made him a constant threat. His speed, technical skill, and goal-scoring ability consistently disrupted opposing defenses. His ability to take on defenders one-on-one and find the back of the net marked him as a true game-changer. Furthermore, Ronaldo's record-breaking goal-scoring feats affirmed his status as one of the greatest footballers in history. His commitment to excellence and unmatched dedication set him apart, providing a significant advantage for his team. There's absolutely no doubt that Ronaldo starts on the left wing for this best XI.

In the 2013/2014 UEFA Champions League season, Gareth Bale's performance as a right winger was nothing short of extraordinary. With 10 goals and assists, he showcased his world-class ability and impact on the team, contributing significantly to his side's success. Bale's blistering pace and powerful shot made him a relentless force, rendering him unplayable at times for opposing defenders. One of the standout moments of that season was his clutch goal in the final. While it may not have been the most spectacular, it was timed perfectly and underscored his ability to deliver when it mattered most, ultimately contributing to his team's triumph. Bale's combination of attributes, including his remarkable pace and power, marked him as a game-changer, ensuring that he remained an absolute force on the pitch. This was Gareth Bale at his peak, and he certainly earns a spot in this best XI.

Ángel Di María's 2013/2014 UEFA Champions League season was defined by his outstanding creativity, clutch performances, and significant contributions. With 6 assists, tying for the season's most, he served as the creative engine of his side, consistently setting up goal-scoring opportunities. What truly set him apart was his ability to shine in critical moments, showcasing his pivotal role in the team's success. Moreover, being tied for 3rd in goal contributions that season highlighted his all-around impact. Di María's versatility and ability to both create and score goals make him a clear choice for this best XI. He stood a level above all other attacking midfielders in this UCL season.

In a season that may not have been characterized by an extraordinary striker performance, Zlatan Ibrahimović still managed to stand out with his solid contribution. Finishing as the second-highest goal-scorer, he proved to be one of the few potent threats in his team's attack. With the third-highest goals per 90 minutes and the second-highest in goal contributions, Zlatan demonstrated his ability to consistently make an impact. Despite the relative challenges, his goal-scoring prowess and all-around offensive contributions were invaluable, making him an essential element in his team's success. Being the best centre forward of the lot this season, Zlatan goes up top in my team.
